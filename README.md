# YTU Video Archive Platform

During the COVID-19 pandemic, courses at universities were conducted fully or partially online. Many universities began storing lecture recordings in their learning management systems (such as Blackboard, Moodle, Canvas-like platforms) as a **video archive**. However, access to these videos is usually limited to students enrolled in the course, making it difficult for graduates or students from different semesters to access them.

This project aims to develop a **student-centered shared video and course material platform** that is accessible **only to students of our university**.

Students can easily share links to online lecture recordings (via YTU’s video archive system). Other students can browse and access these videos by department or course, allowing them to revisit lectures. Additionally, materials such as **PDFs, slides, and notes** can also be shared for theoretical courses.

The project aims to create a **sustainable environment for student collaboration and knowledge sharing**, even beyond the pandemic. It will be especially useful during exam periods, in cases of absence, or when students struggle to understand certain topics.

---

## Motivation and Problem Statement

* **Pandemic Legacy**: High-quality online lecture recordings from the COVID period and beyond should not be lost; all students should benefit from them.
* **Inequality of Access**: Not everyone takes the same course from the same instructor with the same quality. One student can help others by sharing a well-explained lecture.
* **Department-Based Organization**: Each department can have its own course repository (e.g., Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Business Administration).
* **Student-Centered Access**: Registration is only possible via university email (@std.yildiz.edu.tr), preventing external access.
* **Additional Benefits**: Students can also share notes, summaries, cheat sheets, and past exam questions.

---

## Core Features

### Basic Features

* **User Registration & Login**: Registration via university email with mandatory verification.
* **Video Sharing**:

  * Course selection (dropdown or search)
  * Semester/Year selection (e.g., Fall 2020–2021, Spring 2021–2022)
  * Adding video links
  * Adding descriptions and tags
* **Department-Based Discovery**:

  * Browse all shared courses within a selected department
  * Explore interesting courses from other departments
* **Course Material Sharing**:

  * Upload PDF, Word, PowerPoint files
  * Attach notes to a specific video or share independently
* **Search & Filtering**:

  * Search by course name, instructor, year, or department
  * Sort by most viewed, newest, or most upvoted content
* **Announcements**:

  * A section where students can share and view internship opportunities
* **Weekly Topic Bulletin**:

  * Course syllabus based on the Bologna system

---

## Use Cases

1. A student shares the video link of the “Data Structures” course from Spring 2022.
2. Another student from the same department searches for “Data Structures” and views all shared videos and notes.
3. A student from Electrical Engineering explores and watches videos from the “Algorithms” course in Computer Engineering.
4. Before exams, students compare lecture videos from different instructors to study more effectively.

---

## Target Audience

* All undergraduate and graduate students within the same university
* Especially students who missed or want to revisit online lectures from the 2020–2023 pandemic period and beyond
* Students interested in elective courses from other departments
